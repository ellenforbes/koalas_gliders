# Pembroke colour palette

The 2018 brand guide and the production EPS artwork differ slightly on the rose's red. This reference uses the **production artwork** as the source of truth.

## Primary colours

### Pembroke Red — the brandmark colour

| Space | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Hex / Web** | **`#D9212C`** | Production EPS artwork |
| RGB | 217, 33, 44 | Production EPS artwork |
| PMS | 1795 | 2018 brand guide |
| CMYK | C13 M96 Y90 K3 | 2018 brand guide |

Use `#D9212C` in digital deliverables. For press, specify **PMS 1795** and let the printer match. Reserved for the Tudor Rose, accent rules, key headlines, and small graphic emphasis. Not for large solid backgrounds.

### Pembroke Charcoal — wordmark and dark background

| Space | Value |
|---|---|
| **Hex / Web** | **`#101921`** |
| RGB | 16, 25, 33 |

Wordmark colour on light and taupe backgrounds (not pure `#000000`). Also the dark-background colour for the dark logo variant. Use for body text on white and for table-header fills.

### Pembroke Taupe — warm neutral background

| Space | Value |
|---|---|
| **Hex / Web** | **`#CEC5C0`** |
| RGB | 206, 197, 192 |

Warm grey/taupe used as a third logo-background variant. Pairs with the charcoal wordmark. Useful for editorial spreads, document covers, and stationery alternatives.

### Pembroke Taupe Light — soft banding tint

| Space | Value |
|---|---|
| **Hex / Web** | **`#F5F2F0`** |
| RGB | 245, 242, 240 |

A very pale tint derived from the taupe. Use for Excel alternating-row banding, soft section backgrounds in Word, and subtle panel fills on the web. Looks neutral but harmonises with the taupe and charcoal.

### White

| Space | Value |
|---|---|
| Hex | `#FFFFFF` |

Wordmark colour on dark backgrounds. Default page colour.

## Charcoal tints (use these instead of arbitrary greys)

| Tint | CMYK | RGB | Hex |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80% | K80 | 88, 89, 91 | `#58595B` |
| 60% | K60 | 128, 130, 132 | `#808284` |
| 40% | K40 | 167, 169, 172 | `#A7A9AC` |
| 20% | K20 | 209, 211, 212 | `#D1D3D4` |

Use 20–40% for decorative lines and secondary backgrounds, 60–80% for secondary text, captions, and metadata.

## Colour spaces by output

- **Print / offset** (letterhead, cards, brochures): specify PMS for spot, CMYK for process / digital print.
- **Screen** (web, app, email, video, Office files): Hex / RGB.
- **PDFs intended for both print and screen**: CMYK is safer.

Do not mix: a single deliverable should use one colour space throughout its source files.

## Chart and data-visualisation palette

When a chart or status indicator needs more than one colour, work down this list in order. Stay on-palette; avoid bright greens, oranges, yellows, magentas.

1. Pembroke Red `#D9212C`
2. Pembroke Charcoal `#101921`
3. 60% Charcoal `#808284`
4. Pembroke Taupe `#CEC5C0`
5. 40% Charcoal `#A7A9AC`
6. 20% Charcoal `#D1D3D4`

For diverging or sequential scales, build them from tints of Charcoal or Taupe rather than introducing new hues.

Koala social network (beacon detections)

When two collared koalas come close enough, each collar detects the other via Bluetooth/RF and logs a beacon event. We've parsed identified pair-detections out of the raw collar data — 268 events across 148 unique pairs. Node size scales with number of distinct partners detected.

Important data limitation: the network below is built from a small fraction of actual koala-to-koala encounters. The collars log roughly 59,000 aggregate beacon events (BM_KOALA_BEACON_NEAR / HEARD / CLOSE_INTERACTION) where two koalas were close enough to be detected, but only 268 of those events (about 0.5%) include the partner's identity. Even worse, 72% of identified events are from March 2026 alone, and 22 of 49 collared koalas have zero identified events despite thousands of anonymous detections. This strongly suggests a firmware or export-configuration change in late 2025 / early 2026 that started including partner IDs in scheduled reports. The network here is real but heavily under-sampled and skewed to recent collars. For epidemiological / sociality work, ask the collar provider whether earlier exports can be re-pulled with partner identities, or use the anonymous BM_KOALA_BEACON_* counts as a proxy for contact rate.

Edge lists are exported to beacon_pairs.csv and beacon_detections.csv for downstream chlamydia / social analysis.
